SONIC DISCOVERY

A Sonic the Hedgehog fan-game created and submitted to the 2021 Sonic Amateur Games Expo (SAGE). 2D momentum-based platformer This was my first released game and second video game developed. During the development process of this game I started realizing that designing levels was an extremely interesting aspect to me. This continued as I worked on more and more projects as I pushed myself to emphasize level design as a core part of my digital experiences. I am still proud of the way I designed the stages for this game.

Solo Project Software: Game Maker Studio 1.4, Paint.NET, Adobe Photoshop, Audacity Roles: Game Designer, Artist, Level Designer Unique Systems: Set Pieces, Gamepad Support

PROJECT DETAILS

Programmed in Game Maker Studio 1.4 using Flicky 8, a Sonic gameplay framework created by F4LK. My Work: Designed & Developed two completely orignal stages with sprawling and replayable level design. Designed & Developed tutorial stages for each of the three playable characters. Created orignal artwork for title screen, menus, gameplay, and UI. Programmed in-game controller support. Developed orignal gimmicks, stage objects, and set pieces. Designed & Developed orignal special stages accesable through checkpoints. Accolades: over 12,000 downloads & stellar reviews for inventive level design, a creative artstyle, and exciting set pieces.

LEVEL DESIGN

Level design is based on the three path philosophy found in early Sonic the Hedgehog games. This will sometimes funnel into a one or two path structure to keep the stages more focused. Evolved by adding more variation with verticallity, set peices, and new gimmicks. Acessing higher routes is achieved by learning the physics and level design by replaying the stage. Higher routes usually reward the player with a faster time and continued flow throughout the stage. These diagrams show various snippets of level design and how I approched designing it.

ARTWORK

I wanted this game to have more of a mix between cartoon aesthetics & realism as I really wanted to sell the feeling of a bustling cityscape. This is all of the sprite art I contributed to the project, most of everything thing else used in the project were assets from Spriter's Resource and old Sonic games. Most of what I worked on was art for the physical geometry: slopes, platforms, etc. and some background assets like signage. I also created various redesigns of existing objects such as springs, ramps, and item monitors.
